Going to an Atlanta Braves game

When considering going to an Atlanta Braves game, there are many factors a person or group of people will need to take into account. First, how to get there. If you do not live in the greater Atlanta area, transportation will have to be arranged. Whether that be driving yourself and your friends or flying, depending on your distance from Atlanta. The second factor to take into account is the cost of tickets as well as the cost of food and beverages once inside the stadium. One final factor to take into account is spending money for merchandise if you want a jersey or a hat.

Now that the financial aspect of going to the game has been accounted for, let’s talk about getting to the game. Atlanta traffic is known for being awful at nearly all times of the day so plan to leave early to being able to combat traffic. Also, be prepared to walk once you park at or near the stadium. Once inside the stadium it is always fun to walk around and explore the stadium and what it has to offer before heading to your seats.

Now that you have gotten to your seats get ready to sit back, relax, and take in the sights, sounds, and smells of the ballpark. Depending on where your seats are located you can look forward to something different from every location. If you are seated in the outfield you can look forward to catching a home run or even a foul ball depending on where you are located within the outfield seats. If you are seated along the baselines, either first or third, you can expect to see people called out or safe right in front of you. Finally, if you are seated behind home plate be prepared for fast balls to come from the pitcher’s mound directly at you! Do not worry, there is a net designed to protect you. Lastly, the most important part of going to a baseball game is to have fun. So, sit back, relax, and enjoy America’s pastime.
